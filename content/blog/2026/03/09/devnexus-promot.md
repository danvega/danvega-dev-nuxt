## Workshop Day: Wednesday, March 4th

Wednesday is workshop day, and we have three great ones lined up.

**Fundamentals of Software Engineering in the Age of AI** is the workshop I'm co-presenting with my good friend Nate Schutta. We wrote a book on the fundamentals of software engineering, and I have a lot to say about where those fundamentals fit in today's world. The AI conversation has changed how we think about the craft of building software, and I love sitting there listening to Nate talk about this stuff. You're in for a real treat.

**Hands-On: Building Agents with Spring AI, MCP, Java, and Amazon Bedrock** is being led by Josh Long and James Ward. If I wasn't running my own workshop, this is the one I would attend. Getting hands-on time building AI agents with Spring AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) is going to be incredibly valuable.

**Leveraging Your Existing API Ecosystem to Power Safe Enterprise AI** rounds out the workshop lineup. If your organization is figuring out how to bring AI into your existing systems without starting from scratch, this is the session for you.

The first full day of sessions covers a wide range of topics. Here are the ones from the Tanzu and Spring team:

- **Life After Dad Jokes: Building Enterprise-Grade AI Frameworks for Java** - This one really sticks out to me. We've all built the "tell me a dad joke" AI integration. But what comes after that? This session tackles real enterprise AI patterns.

- **Modern API Versioning with Spring Cloud Gateway**
- **Beautiful Spring Security**
- **The Fundamentals of Software Engineering in the Age of AI** - If you missed the full workshop on Wednesday, this is the one-hour abridged version. Come check it out.
- **Building AI Agents with Spring and MCP**
- **Building AI Agents with Spring AI on the Tanzu Platform** - The best place to publish your AI services.

## The Atlanta Experience

If you haven't been to DevNexus before, the Georgia World Congress Center is in a great part of Atlanta. We usually stay at the AC Hotel downtown, which is right across the street. To get there, you walk through Olympic Park. The Olympics were in Atlanta back in 1996, and the park still has the Olympic rings on display. It's a really walkable area with great restaurants and plenty to do outside of the conference.
